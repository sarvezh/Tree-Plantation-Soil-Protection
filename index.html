Why Tree Plantation & Soil Protection Matter

Trees and healthy soil are fundamental to our planet's ecosystem. Understanding their importance is the first step toward effective conservation.

The Environmental Crisis

Our planet faces unprecedented environmental challenges. With 4.7 million hectares of forest lost annually and 92.4 million hectares affected by soil erosion in India alone, immediate action is crucial.

Trees serve as nature's air purifiers, carbon storage systems, and soil protectors. A single mature tree can absorb 48 pounds of CO2 annually and cool urban areas by up to 10¬∞F.

  • Nearly 50% of terrestrial biodiversity resides in forests
  • Trees reduce stormwater runoff by 15-27% of annual rainfall
  • Urban forests can improve air quality by up to 30%
  • Tree roots prevent soil erosion and improve water infiltration
